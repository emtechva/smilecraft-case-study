01 · The Problem

Manual booking and scattered follow‑up create chaos

Without automation, dental clinics rely on phone calls, sticky notes, and disconnected tools. Appointments slip through the cracks, rescheduling is reactive, and long‑term patient relationships fade.

  • Missed reminders — No‑shows increase because patients forget appointments.
  • Disjointed follow‑up — Cancelled or no‑show patients rarely receive a timely rebooking invitation.
  • No single source of truth — Staff can't see at a glance who booked, attended, cancelled, or needs a follow‑up.
  • Manual status updates — After every appointment, someone has to update spreadsheets or CRM records manually.
The core challenge: A dental practice needs a system that captures appointments online, automatically communicates with patients, and organises every outcome so nothing gets forgotten.

03 · Front‑End Funnel

How patients book online

Three connected pages convert a website visitor into a confirmed appointment — no phone tag required.

Landing Page Introduces the clinic, lists services, and builds enough trust to move the visitor toward the booking page.
Booking Page The patient picks a date and time using the GoHighLevel calendar. The appointment is instantly created in the CRM.
Thank‑You Page Confirms the booking and sets expectations for the next steps (reminders, visit details).
04 · Backend Automation

How the system keeps the appointment on track

Once a booking is made, four workflows fire automatically — handling reminders, status collection, outcome routing, and long‑term nurture.

1
Appointment Confirmation & Reminders
Triggers immediately after booking
1
Booking fires the workflow.
2
The contact is tagged and moved to "Appointment Booked" in the CRM pipeline.
3
A confirmation email with appointment details is sent instantly.
4
1‑day and 4‑hour reminders go out automatically to reduce no‑shows.
5
After the appointment time, the clinic receives an internal status‑check message.
2
Appointment Status Trigger
Staff selects the outcome with one click
1
The internal message contains three Trigger Links: Attended, Cancelled, No‑Show.
2
Staff clicks the relevant link. No manual CRM editing needed.
3
The link launches the correct downstream workflow automatically.
3
Outcome Routing & Rescheduling
Different paths for attended, cancelled, and no‑show
1
Attended: Patient moves to "Attended" and receives a thank‑you + feedback email.
2
Cancelled: Tagged, moved to "Cancelled", and sent a rescheduling email immediately.
3
No‑Show: Moved to "No‑Show" and sent a recovery email with a direct rebooking link.
4
If the patient doesn't rebook right away, they get a "needs‑reschedule" tag and enter the nurture sequence.
4
Patient Nurture Email Workflow
Long‑term follow‑up for unbooked patients
1
Sends an educational email with dental tips and a booking link.
2
Waits 7 days, then sends a gentle follow‑up.
3
Waits another 30 days and sends a final reconnection reminder.
